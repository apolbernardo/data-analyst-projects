# Camera Sales Analysis (Fujifilm Case Study)

## Project Overview
This project analyzes sales performance, product trends, and customer behavior within the camera retail industry, using Fujifilm as a case study. Through RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis, the project identifies high-value customers and purchasing patterns to generate actionable business insights that support decision-making in retail operations and marketing strategies.

## Tools & Techniques
- Python (Pandas, NumPy)
- Data Visualization (Matplotlib, Seaborn)
- RFM Analysis for customer segmentation
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

## Key Insights
- **High-Value Customers:** Identified a small segment of customers who bought recently, purchase frequently, and spent the most, contributing a large portion of total revenue.  
- **Big Spenders:** Some customers with high monetary value but lower purchase frequency could benefit from targeted promotions to increase repeat purchases.  
- **At-Risk Customers:** Customers who haven’t purchased in a long time but had previously spent moderately are at risk of churn, highlighting opportunities for win-back campaigns.  
- **Purchase Patterns:** Peak sales months were identified, with certain products like Instax cameras being top sellers, which can guide inventory and marketing planning.  
- **Actionable Recommendations:** Focus on loyalty programs for best customers, promotional campaigns for big spenders, win-back strategies for at-risk customers, and seasonal marketing to maximize revenue.
